Introduction

You wanna make a logo right? For this demonstration I will use open source application GIMP (GNU Image Manipulation Program). It's a free software raster graphics editor. And it is cross platform, it means it can be used on Linux, Windows and MAC OS X. You can download it from this link. If you don't have it, then go and grab it from there.

So, if you have GIMP then open it up. After that we will create our first logo with it.

Wrap up

I assume, you already opened GIMP, so at first we are going to make a new file, so when it asks for width and height for this image make it as 1024x1024 (widthxheight) and click on the advanced option and choose 300ppi

for X and Y resolution. Then click OK.

Now we need guide ruler for our convenience. So, click on image option from the title/menu bar then navigate to Guides and choose New Guides (by Percent)

. A dialogue box will appear, then choose Horizontal direction, and for position (in percent) choose 50 and then click ok. Again navigatte to Guides (New Guides by Percent) and choose vertical option and for position choose 50 in percent. You will see two guides(horizontal and vertical) appeared on the image.

Now select Ellipse Select Tool then, choose Expand from center and Fixed from the bottom option. After that move your mouse to center of the image where two guides are joins together. Now click on it and expand your circle until the circle covers up your image.
